Assignment One: As We May Think

I went into this article not knowing what to expect, and so was initially
very confused about the perspective. I probably should've noticed the fact
that it was from 1945, but it was almost more interesting to go in blind.
It discussed topics such as science, photography, and data collection and
management in such archaic terms, and imagined improvements that already
currently exist. It was actually interesting to listen to the author parse
through the improvements of their time. To us now, both the author's view
of what is old technology and what is new technology is so outdated that
it's not something we really consider. However, for the author, it is such
a novice concept to be able to do things such as "dry photography" and being
able to compress Encyclopedias into a few pages. The way in which he imagines
possible improvements for the future and almost takes humor in it the same
way we do with concepts like flying cars is such a sobering experience. It
really puts into perspective how quickly and suddenly we can evolve and
innovate and what an impact it has on us. Things that were once considered
jokes are now accepted as completely normal and we continue to come up with
and implement further advancements that get more and more complex as we figure
out how to compress and delegate the once time-consuming tasks to machines of
our own making. It really gets you thinking about what the next generation's
new "norm" will be.
